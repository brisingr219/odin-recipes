Description

Baked potato soup is a dish that we typically make here at my house on those chilly Autumn nights. It is a simple and cost-effective way to make a filling meal that is guaranteed to be loved by all.

Ingredients

  • 10-11 Medium to Large Sized Russet Potatoes
  • 1/4 Teaspoon Salt
  • 16 Slices of Bacon
  • 1 Large Yellow Onion
  • 6 Cloves of Garlic
  • 2 Tablespoons of Butter
  • 1/4 Cup Flour
  • 3 1/2 Cups Chicken Broth
  • 2 Cups Half and Half
  • 1/4 Cup of Sour Cream
  • 1/2 Teaspoon of Black Pepper
  • 2 Cups of Shredded Cheddar Cheese
  • 1 Bundle of Fresh Chives
  • Onion Powder
  • Garlic Powder

Directions

  1. Start by washing and peeling your potatoes, then cut them into one inch sized cubes.
  2. Put the cut potatoes into a brazier and fill it with water so that it just covers the potatoes, then start heating that up to a boil.
  3. Now take your bacon and slice them into cubes about a half inch wide
  4. Dice your onion and mince your garlic and set them to the side for now
  5. Your chives need to be finely diced, you'll want small pieces for your garnish.
  6. While your potatoes are boiling we'll take a large frying pan and start cooking the bacon. Note: please observe SAFE food handling procedures, always wash your hands after touching raw meats and clean any surfaces you may place them on thoroughly to prevent any cross contamination.
  7. When your bacon is dark and crisp transfer it to either a plate lines with paper towels or a draining rack . This is to let the grease from the bacon drain a bit so they aren't sopping wet.
  8. Now take two tablespoons of the bacon grease in the pan and set them in a small bowl or cup to the side. We'll be using this later.
  9. While we're waiting for the potatoes to finish boiling lets grab another pot, I use a stock pot, and let add a little bit of cooking oil and the bacon grease we saved to the bottom so we can start sauteing our onions and garlic.
  10. On a medium-medium high heat let the onions cook down until they are nice and soft before adding your garlic.
  11. When your veg are cooked to a sufficient degree you'll want to toss your butter into the pot with them. Once this is mostly melted down reduce your heat to a medium-low.
  12. Check your potatoes, you want a fork to pierce the potato easily, but not so easy that it splits apart without much force.
  13. After your butter is melted fully we are going to slowly add our flour. Using a silicone whisk or spatula mix your flour slowly into the butter and vegetables. If done properly this will leave you with a paste-like mixture. This is vital for thickening the soup in the next step.
  14. Now we will add the chicken broth while mixing slowly. Once finished you will slowly add your half and half while stirring the soup. Be careful you don't dump it all in at once, be patient and take it slow.
  15. Once your potatoes have reached the desired tenderness, remove from heat and strain the potatoes. Once done transfer them to a large mixing bowl and lightly mash them. You want large chunks of potato for the soup but softened so it all mixes well.
  16. One done mashing add the potatoes to the soup and stir well to homogenize the potatoes with the soup.
  17. Now we will add the onion powder, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper to the soup to taste.
  18. Once finished mixing bring everything up to a boil and reduce to a low heat. Once lowered we are going to mix in our cheese.
  19. After that is complete you're ready to serve!
  20. Scoop the soup into a bowl and then sprinkle some of the bacon and chives over top. Feel free to add a dollop of sour cream and some extra cheese if you'd like.
  21. Now dig in!
