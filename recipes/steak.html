Description

This dish is one typically saved in my household for a special occasion. It does not tend to be the kindest on your checkbook, but when completed it is well worth the money you spend for the ingredients. It is a pan seared New York strip steak, served with oven baked asparagus, and air fried baby golden potatoes. I have found that learning how to make this dish properly really strengthens and builds on a lot of the foundations and skills you learn when you're first starting out on your cooking journey, and developing new ones that will increase your skill. Please keep in mind this is not a beginner's dish. It is going to be tough, it is going to test your patience, but at the end of it all you will have grown as a cook! Enough yammering from me though. Let's get to it!

Ingredients

  • 3 New York Strip Steaks
  • Two Bushels of Asparagus
  • 3 lbs of Baby Gold Yukon potatoes
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Fresh Thyme
  • Butter
  • Cooking Oil
  • Parmesan Cheese
  • Four Cloves of Garlic (per steak)
  • Onion Powder
  • Garlic Powder
  • Dried Oregano
  • Dried Basil
  • Dried Rosemary
  • Cajun Seasoning (optional)

Directions

  1. We'll start by preheating our oven to 425 degrees Fahrenheit. This is for the asparagus
  2. I use an air fryer for my potatoes. My settings are 400 degrees Fahrenheit for 20 minutes, shaking the potatoes around at the 10 minutes.
  3. Let's also get out a pan to cook our steak in. The best kind is one big enough to fit the steak comfortably, while leaving about an inch and a half of room on all sides. This ensures you don't overcrowd the pan, and leave well enough room to baste the steaks later.
  4. Now let's prep the separate parts of the dish!
  5. For the asparagus you'll want to make sure you clean them and then break the ends off at their natural points. Note: This can be done by grabbing either end of the asparagus and bending it until it breaks on it's own
  6. For the potatoes we are going to quarter them. They should be fairly small, and you'll cut them in half once and then in half once more.
  7. For the steaks you'll want to make sure that after they are defrosted you let them rest at room temp for at least 10-15 minutes before you apply any of the seasonings. This is a pretty good time to pat the steaks dry with a paper towel, removing any of the moisture build up from the defrosting process. Let's get to seasoning! Note: please observe SAFE food handling procedures, always wash your hands after touching raw meats and clean any surfaces you may place them on thoroughly to prevent any cross contamination.
  8. In a large mixing bowl toss your quartered potatoes. Over top we'll add a thin layer of fresh cracked salt, a thin layer of fresh cracked pepper, oregano, basil, rosemary, onion powder, and garlic powder.
  9. Now toss the potatoes kind of like a salad. This is to spread and mix the seasonings and oil so they cover all the potato sections evenly. Taste your mixture afterwards If you feel that there's ever not enough of a certain seasoning add more. However, you'll want to do this very carefully. Seasonings can be added easily, but overseasoning is a real possibility and can be tricky to undo.
  10. After your potatoes are seasoned we'll transfer them to the baskets of the air fryer. Mine has two 5 quart baskets so it makes cooking large batches at the same time fairly easy. (Ninja for the win! lol)
  11. Next, in the same bowl as the potatoes, add the asparagus. You will use a similar process for seasoning. But we are not using any of the dried spices. So we'll add the oil, salt, pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder. Again tasting as we go.
  12. Now we will toss the asparagus onto a baking sheet lined with aluminum (aluminium for my friends across the pond), and add a layer of Parmesan cheese over-top the asparagus. Either ground or shredded cheese is good for this. Note: Unless you are only cooking one steak, refrain from throwing the asparagus into the oven quite yet. We'll add them in once we get to our second or third steak because they will only be cooking for twelve to fifteen minutes and you'll want them as warm as possible when you're plating your dish.
  13. Now we'll toss about a tablespoon or two's worth of cooking oil into our frying pan and get it warming up over a medium heat.
  14. Now let's move to the steaks. As stated above, we are using freshly cracked salt and pepper to this dish. You'll want to add a pretty good layer of salt and pepper to each side of the steaks. Afterwards you'll want to crush your garlic, leaving it either in the peel or removing the peel. It works either way. Note: It has been said by a fairly famous Chef, that approximately 30-50% of the seasonings on the surface of the steak will burn off once the steaks are introduced to the pan. So put more than you think you'll need. I promise it'll end up fine as long as you can still see the color of the steak underneath the seasoning.
  15. Once you have finished seasoning the steaks and your cooking oil has a nice sheen and is at it's smoking point, we'll add the steak to the pan. Note: Lay the steak down away from you, this is to prevent as much oil splash as possible and reduce the chance of any pesky burns.
  16. The key to cooking these steaks is getting a nice dark brown crust on either side and then basting the steaks in butter to finish the process off.
  17. We will let the steak cook on this first side for about two minutes, once you have seared it well you will take some tongs and lift the steak up onto the fat cap present on the side, you will let this portion cook for about a minute to render the fat down and make it possible to eat without the fat being rock solid. This is a crucial step in the cooking process.
  18. After your fat has rendered down properly you will lay the steak on the other flat side to finish off our searing process.
  19. Once the steak has seared on this side for about a minute and a half you'll add about 2 to 3 tablespoons of butter on one side of the pan.
  20. Now lay your garlic and thyme on top of the steak. Push the steak to the side of the pan furthest from you and tip the pan towards you. Then, using a spoon scoop the melted butter over top of the steak. There are countless videos online detailing how to do this, and a visual aid really is the best way to learn this process, so I recommend looking a few videos up to get a good grasp on this skill.
  21. Baste the steak for about a minute to a minute and a half
  22. After the basting process is complete you'll want to lay it on either a clean cutting board or a plate to let it rest for at least five minutes.
  23. After removing it from the pan temp test the steak. Depending on what kind of cook you want the temps vary, but I always try to shoot for a medium rare-medium, which sits somewhere between 125-130 immediately after taking the steak off of heat.Note: while resting the steak will continue cooking, so if it raises a few degrees do not be alarmed! This is normal and will not mess up anything, promise.
  24. Repeat this process (steps 15-23) for the rest of the steaks if you have any.
  25. Plating the dish is as simple as cutting the steak against the grain to your desired thickness, and adding it to a plate with your asparagus and potatoes.
  26. I use the optional Cajun seasoning just before step 25, tossing the cooked potatoes in a medium sized bowl to give them a bit of kick.
  27. And you're done! It was a long process, and no doubt your multi-tasking skills have been pressed to the absolute max but you did it. I hope you enjoy your meal and add it to your repertoire.

Just remember, steak is a dish that is NOT perfected in one go. Like anything getting the process down takes practice, and lots of it. My first ten steaks were not perfect, and the next ten after that were just getting palatable. The times and temps you cook it at are entirely dependent on your specific oven and pan you use, and the specifications given above may need some adjusting. But you'll get it, just keep cooking til you get it right and add this fantastic dish to your repertoire. You won't regret it.
